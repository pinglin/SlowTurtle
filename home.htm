Academic Bio

Since 2010, I have joined the Department of Computing at Imperial College London as a PhD student supervised by Dr. Philip "Eddie" Edwards and Prof. Andrew Davison. My mainstream research is to apply dense visual SLAM to augmented reality in robotic image-guided surgery.



From 2009 to 2010, I did my MSc in Artificial Intelligence at the School of Electronics and Computer Science at the University of Southampton. My mentor was Prof. Mark S Nixon, and my thesis entitled "Learning Human Behaviour in Complex Scenes" was awarded with distinction.

I have been greatly appreciating what Alan Perlis said: "A year spent in artificial intelligence is enough to make one believe in God."
